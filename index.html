Pomelos are quite husky. They have a very thick pith and peel. The colour of the outer rind ranges from a pale green to a bright yellow. The rind has a smooth to pebbly texture. The inner flesh ranges from white to pink to yellow to red and contains a fair amount of seeds.
Pomelos are sweet and tart in taste. Pink and red varieties are sweeter than white varieties. They tend to be sweeter and less acidic overall in comparison to a grapefruit. Pomelos have a distinct floral aroma to their skin as well.

Sodium 6.1mg

Fibre 6g

Contains potassium and folic acid.
Pomelos are an excellent source of Vitamin C which is a powerful antioxidants. Antioxidants like Naringenin and Naringin are immune system boosters and help protect against chronic diseases. The Fibre content in Pomelos aids in digestion and promotes the growth of healthy gut bacteria. The Potassium helps regulate blood pressure as well.

Citrons are generally larger, measuring 12-15 cm in length. They have Yellow-ish skin and a thick flashy rind with a small amount of flesh/pulp. They tend to be oblong or oval in shape.
Citrons have a mild sweet pith unlike most citrus fruit. The pulp can be acidic or sweet or even nearly absent in taste, while the juice can sometimes taste sour. The thick rind emits a highly aromatic scent that can be described as crisp, floral, and fresh.

Papedas are small measuring 4-5 cm in diameter. They are generally oval or oblong in shape with a flattened appearance. They have tough skin that is deeply creased and wrinkled. The surface is also rough and pebbled. The peel ripens from dark green to yellow or orange at maturity. The rind is white and spongey. The core of the papeda is packed with large seeds.
Papedas have sour and bitter skin. The piths are also bitter. The flesh is dry and pulpy accompanied with unpleasant tart, sour, acidic or semi-bitter flavour. But the scent of the papeda can be described as refreshing as it is reminiscent of a floral and bright lemony aroma.
